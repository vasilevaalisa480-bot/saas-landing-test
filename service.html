How it works in a clinic

A straight path from first contact to follow-up—visible to staff who need it, without noisy alerts for everyone.

1

Patient submits a request

Web form, phone, or messenger—requests land in one queue with source tags so reception can respond in order.

2

Administrator books in CRM

The right doctor, room, and slot are chosen from live availability; the patient card is created or updated in one place.

3

Reminders go out automatically

The system sends timed reminders and prep instructions; staff only step in when something needs a human touch.

4

Leadership reviews visits and sources

Reports show completed visits, no-shows, and channel performance so you can adjust marketing and staffing calmly.

Frequently asked questions

Straight answers about integration, scale, training, and go-live—before you commit to a pilot.

Can we connect the CRM to our online booking on the website?

Yes. You can embed or link a booking flow so new requests appear directly in the CRM, with source tracking preserved for reporting.

Do you support multiple branches?

Multiple locations can run on one account with shared patient records where policy allows, per-site calendars, and consolidated or filtered reports for regional managers.

Will our front desk need heavy training?

The interface is built around reception workflows—booking, search, and reminders—not generic sales jargon. Most teams are productive after a short guided session plus quick-reference materials.

How fast can we go live?

A typical pilot runs on the order of a few weeks: import or start fresh with patients, configure services and reminders, then train key users. Exact timing depends on data cleanup and integrations.

What happens to our data if we leave?

You can export structured data under your agreement, and we provide a clear offboarding window so nothing feels rushed or opaque.
